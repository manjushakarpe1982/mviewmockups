How to read these numbers

Recorded facts: production volumes, operators, well status, and your ownership percentages come straight from Texas Railroad Commission filings and county records.

Estimates: the dollar values are Mineral View's valuation model (updated July 6, 2026). The model projects each well's future production from its actual decline history and applies market gas-price forecasts. It is an estimate of value — not an appraisal, and not an offer. Real sale prices depend on your lease terms, title, and the buyer.

Plain-English glossary for this report

  • Mcf — a thousand cubic feet of natural gas. The standard unit on royalty statements.
  • Royalty interest — your percentage share of what a well produces, paid without you paying drilling or operating costs.
  • Lease / tract — the state tracks production by lease number. One "unit" (like Simmons) can span several numbered tracts.
  • Decline — every well produces less over time. A gentle decline like your Clear Creek wells' is normal and expected.
